A gas giant is a large planet composed mostly of gases, such as hydrogen and helium, with a relatively small rocky core. The gas giants of our solar system are Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. These four planets are also called Jovian planets. Jupiter is the largest planet in our solarsystem, it has a radius eleven times that of earth the planet is mainly made up of hydrogen and helium. Jupiter also has fifty moons and seventeen not confirmed it is also visable by the human eye. Jupiter is about nine times the radius of earth.

Saturn is characterized by the rings that surround it which is made of water ice and rocky materials and other small particles. Saturn has fifty three moons and nine awaiting to be confirmed. What the planet is made of and the atmosphere are very simaler of that of Jupiter. Uranus has a radius of about four times that of earths and is the only planet tilted on it's side. Uranus also rotates backward compared to all of the other planets except Venus. Neptune has a radius that is four times that of earth and it's atmoshpere is made up of mainly hydrogen helium and methane. Neptune only has 13 confirmed moons.

Many scientists believe that gas giants started out as terrestrial planets but the size of the cores allowed these allied planets to grab hydrogen and helium out from the gas cloud which the sun had stored, before the sun was formed.
